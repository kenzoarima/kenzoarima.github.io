Google AMP Roadshow SG 2017

It was an exciting day at Google as we checked out the new features rolled out by the AMP project team in the recent months! Before everything else, we started by filling up our tanks with sumptuous breakfast and aromatic coffee.

A good day starts with good coffee (and breakfast)!

Pano shot by Joey Rozier (https://twitter.com/mrjoro/status/916127492183752704)

It was a fairly packed auditorium as folks from various companies, including SPH, Tech in Asia and etc, as well as folks from other countries like Malaysia were there too. Everyone was there to find out what is coming up next for AMP.

Paul Bakaus opened the day with an introduction of what Google AMP is about, the project’s status timeline so far, and highlights of how various websites’ revenue have improved as they AMP-ified their webpages.

New things in AMP

Ooh yea~ we can do snowfall on AMP now!

AMP-ifying a webpage used to mean that one has to forgo zany cool features that one could have on a normal webpage — sleek animations, rich interactive components powered with Javascript codes. But this is no longer the case now! Joey Rozier showed us how the AMP project has grown with a good library of components that one can use to make one’s site interactive, as well as design really cool parallax scrolling, 3D animated features by using the <amp-animation> and <amp-position-observer> components! We can check them out at www.ampstart.com.

Some of the other cool features mentioned include these components (picture above), which allows web developers to build more interactive components for the various use cases, such as newsletter sign up forms, live blogs, and autocomplete search boxes.

An amp component for housing analytics scripts from the various vendors.

AB testing using !

Additionally, web analytics and AB testing features are available too!

AMP Ads

Katharina, who works on the Global Publisher Platforms team at Google, then took the stage and introduced us to AMP Ads. Just as the tagline has articulated, AMP Ads strives to proliferate the creation of better and faster ads, so as to tackle the 3 main jarring issues ads are posing to users: disruptive, slow loading, and unsafe ads.

AMP Ads uses 80% less battery than normal ads!

It is amazing how much faster AMP Ads load as compared to normal ads, and even conserves battery life too!

Various companies have also begun adopting AMP Ads in their products.

Some thoughts

A fruitful session where developers engage with the AMP team on stage.

To sum up, with the introduction of these many new components in the AMP project to cater to the needs of the developers to build rich, interactive, and performant websites with good user experience, and the efforts in balancing customer satisfaction and reaping business revenue through AMP Ads, I am grateful to Google in their drive to take care of these many stakeholders needs, all at the same time.

I asked Paul Bakaus, what were his opinions when some people described AMP as a deviation from the web, as a closed ecosystem meant to divide itself from the regular webpages. Paul’s reply was insightful: the web has seen itself being abused/misused by people who capitalised on this vast-reaching and powerful platform for their own gain, at the expense of the general public users. AMP is trying to solve this by establishing rules that protect the user experience. Compared to Facebook’s Instant Articles and Apple News, AMP being an open source project, sets its sights to merge itself into the W3C standards, so AMP components will become native web components like some of the HTML5 DOM elements (e.g. <video>, <audio>, <input type=date>). This grand vision is awe-inspring to me.

Here’s a toast to Google AMP team, and may the web be great again!
